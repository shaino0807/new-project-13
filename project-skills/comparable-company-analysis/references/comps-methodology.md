# Comparable Company Analysis Methodology

## Peer Selection

Choose peers using a funnel:

1. Same primary industry and business model.
2. Similar revenue drivers, end markets, and product mix.
3. Similar geography, regulation, customer concentration, and cyclicality.
4. Similar size, growth, profitability, leverage, and capital intensity.
5. Public listing with usable trading data and financial disclosure.

Flag weak peers rather than silently excluding them when the listed universe is small.

## Core Metrics

Enterprise value:

`EV = market capitalization + total debt + preferred equity + minority interest - cash and equivalents`

Trading multiples:

`EV/revenue = enterprise value / revenue`

`EV/EBITDA = enterprise value / EBITDA`

`P/E = share price / EPS` or `equity value / net income`

Margin and growth:

`EBITDA margin = EBITDA / revenue`

`Revenue growth = current-period revenue / prior-period revenue - 1`

Use the same period across peers where possible. Label mixed-period data explicitly.

## Percentiles

Calculate valuation range for each multiple across the peer set:

- 25th percentile: downside or lower-quartile peer valuation.
- Median: central peer valuation.
- 75th percentile: upper-quartile peer valuation.

Exclude negative or not meaningful multiples from percentile calculations, and explain the exclusion.

## Implied Valuation

For target company financials:

`Implied EV from EV/revenue = target revenue * selected EV/revenue multiple`

`Implied EV from EV/EBITDA = target EBITDA * selected EV/EBITDA multiple`

`Implied equity value from P/E = target net income * selected P/E multiple`

Bridge enterprise value to equity value when needed:

`Equity value = enterprise value - net debt - preferred equity - minority interest`

`Implied share price = equity value / diluted shares outstanding`

## Premium And Discount Framework

Use premium arguments when the target has:

- Faster and more durable revenue growth.
- Higher EBITDA margin or better incremental margin.
- Stronger market position, switching costs, brand, technology, or network effects.
- Lower leverage, cleaner balance sheet, and better cash conversion.
- More recurring revenue or less cyclicality than peers.

Use discount arguments when the target has:

- Slower growth, weaker profitability, or margin volatility.
- Smaller scale, customer concentration, weaker liquidity, or governance concerns.
- Higher leverage, high capital intensity, regulatory risk, or execution risk.
- Lower disclosure quality or less diversified business mix.

## Report Style

Keep tables decision-useful rather than exhaustive. Round financial statement values sensibly and use `x` for multiples.

For Chinese reports, use:

- Comparable company analysis: `可比公司分析`
- Peer companies: `同業公司`
- Enterprise value: `企業價值`
- Market capitalization: `市值`
- Revenue: `營收`
- EBITDA margin: `EBITDA 利潤率`
- EV/revenue: `EV/營收`
- P/E: `本益比`
- Implied valuation: `隱含估值`
