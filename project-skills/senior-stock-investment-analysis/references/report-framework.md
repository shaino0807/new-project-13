# Senior Stock Investment Analysis Report Framework

## Data Collection Checklist

Before producing a final report, gather:

| Category | Data Needed |
|---|---|
| Market data | Current price, market cap, enterprise value, shares outstanding, valuation date |
| Income statement | Revenue, gross profit, operating income, EBITDA, net income, diluted EPS |
| Balance sheet | Cash and short-term investments, total debt, total equity, current assets, current liabilities |
| Cash flow | Operating cash flow, capital expenditures, free cash flow, dividends, buybacks |
| Valuation | P/E, P/S, P/B, EV/EBITDA, five-year company averages, peer multiples, industry averages |
| Business | Segment revenue, geographic mix, major customers, products, pricing model |
| Governance | CEO, management tenure, board composition, insider ownership, compensation alignment |

## Core Formulas

| Metric | Formula |
|---|---|
| Revenue growth | Current period revenue / prior period revenue - 1 |
| Gross margin | Gross profit / revenue |
| Operating margin | Operating income / revenue |
| Net margin | Net income / revenue |
| Debt-to-equity | Total debt / total equity |
| Current ratio | Current assets / current liabilities |
| Free cash flow | Operating cash flow - capital expenditures |
| P/E | Market price per share / diluted EPS |
| P/S | Market capitalization / revenue |
| P/B | Market capitalization / book value of equity |
| EV/EBITDA | Enterprise value / EBITDA |

Use consistent signs for capital expenditures. If capex is reported as a cash outflow, calculate FCF as operating cash flow plus reported capex, or convert capex to a positive spending number and subtract it.

The valuation conclusion should not rely on a single multiple. Explain which multiples are most relevant for the business model. For example, EV/EBITDA may be more useful for leveraged or capital-intensive companies, while P/S may be relevant for high-growth companies with depressed earnings.

## Analytical Standards

Prefer a hold rating when evidence is mixed or valuation already prices in reasonable upside. Use buy only when upside is supported by valuation, growth durability, financial health, and identifiable catalysts. Use sell when valuation, fundamentals, balance sheet risk, or competitive erosion create an unfavorable risk-reward balance.

Avoid treating recent price momentum as a thesis by itself. Connect every recommendation to cash flows, earnings power, balance sheet resilience, competitive advantage, and valuation.
